# LethalMalfunctions

> *"ATTENTION ALL EMPLOYEES: The Company has recently conducted a budget review. We have determined that replacing the ship's 40-year-old wiring is not a cost-effective strategy at this time. As a result, you may experience occasional, spontaneous, and potentially life-threatening system failures during your orbital flights. Do not panic. Simply reboot the systems via the main terminal. Remember: Oxygen is a privilege, meeting the quota is mandatory."*

**LethalMalfunctions** transforms your cozy, safe ship into a ticking time bomb of cheap corporate engineering. 

The Company refused to pay for maintenance, so now you have to do it yourself. During your expeditions, various systems on your ship will randomly break down, forcing someone to stay back and manage the chaos via the ship's Terminal.

### ⚠️ Current Malfunctions
*   **Power Outages (`lights`):** The ship's main breaker will frequently short out, plunging the entire cabin into total darkness.
*   **Hydraulic Jams (`doors`):** The hydraulic fluid in the ship's doors is mostly made of cheap soy sauce now. Doors will jam shut, trapping you inside or outside.
*   **Radar Interference (`radar`):** The main monitor will turn to static, leaving the operator completely blind to monsters and loot.
*   **Comms Failure (`comms`):** Signal translators and walkie-talkies will emit deafening static, cutting off all communication with the crew inside the facility.

### 🛠️ How to Repair
When a system breaks, the ship's terminal operator must type the repair command into the terminal to reboot the hardware.

Type `repair [system]` to fix it.
*   `repair lights`
*   `repair doors`
*   `repair radar`
*   `repair comms`

You can also check the current status of the ship's hardware by typing `status` in the terminal.
