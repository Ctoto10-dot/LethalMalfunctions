# LethalMalfunctions

> *"ATTENTION ALL EMPLOYEES: The Company has recently conducted a budget review. We have determined that replacing the ship's 40-year-old wiring is not a cost-effective strategy at this time. As a result, you may experience occasional, spontaneous, and potentially life-threatening system failures during your orbital flights. Do not panic. Simply reboot the systems via the main terminal or the physical breaker box. Remember: Oxygen is a privilege, meeting the quota is mandatory."*

**LethalMalfunctions** transforms your cozy, safe ship into a ticking time bomb of cheap corporate engineering. 

The Company refused to pay for maintenance, so now you have to do it yourself. During your expeditions, various critical systems on your ship will randomly break down, forcing the crew to coordinate manual repairs both via the Terminal and physical interactions around the ship.

---

### ⚠️ Known Ship Malfunctions & Repair Procedures

#### 💡 1. Power Outages (`lights`)
The ship's main breaker will frequently short out, plunging the entire cabin into total darkness.
*   **Repair Procedure:** You cannot fix this via the terminal! A crew member must walk up to the physical **Ship Breaker Box** on the wall and manually flip the Main Lights Breaker (Switch #0) back to ON.

#### 🚪 2. Hydraulic Jams (`doors`)
The hydraulic fluid in the ship's blast doors is mostly made of cheap soy sauce now. Doors will jam shut or open, trapping you inside or outside.
*   **Repair Procedure:** The terminal operator must type `repair doors` to flush the hydraulic lines.

#### 📡 3. Comms Failure (`comms`)
Signal translators and walkie-talkies will emit deafening static, cutting off all communication with the crew inside the facility.
*   **Repair Procedure:** A crew member must physically climb onto the **roof of the ship** and manually interact with the sparking Comms Antenna. 
*   **WARNING:** You MUST turn OFF the Comms Breaker (Switch #3) on the wall before touching the antenna, or you will be severely electrocuted!

#### 🖥️ 4. Radar Interference (`radar`)
The main monitor will turn to static, leaving the operator completely blind to monsters and loot.
*   **Repair Procedure:** A crew member must look at the physical **Ship Breaker Box** on the wall. The box will display a 4-digit calibration code. The terminal operator must type `repair radar` followed by that code to recalibrate the screen.

#### 💻 5. Terminal Glitch (`terminal`)
The terminal itself will crash and become completely unresponsive. 
*   **Repair Procedure:** You cannot use the terminal to fix the terminal! A crew member must manually cycle the Terminal Breaker (Switch #5) OFF and ON on the physical Ship Breaker Box to force a hard reboot.

---

### ⚡ Experimental Defense Grid (Shocker)
If monsters breach the ship, the terminal operator can type `shocker` to activate the ship's experimental defense grid. 
*   **REQUIREMENT:** The shocker will ONLY work if ALL ship systems are fully operational. If even one system (lights, doors, etc.) is broken, the defense grid cannot draw enough power and will fail to activate.
*   **Effect:** This will instantly electrocute any monsters (and players!) standing inside the ship cabin.
*   **Warning:** Activating the shocker causes a massive power surge. All ship systems will go offline for 15 seconds, and all breakers will trip. The ship must reboot before taking off.

---

### 🧰 Summary of Terminal Commands
Type `lm` or `status` to view the current ship systems status.
Type `repair [system]` to fix a system.
Type `shocker` for emergency defense.
